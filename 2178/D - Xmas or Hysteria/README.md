## Problem Statement

<div class="header"><div class="title">D. Xmas or Hysteria</div><div class="time-limit"><div class="property-title">time limit per test</div>2 seconds</div><div class="memory-limit"><div class="property-title">memory limit per test</div>512 megabytes</div><div class="input-file input-standard"><div class="property-title">input</div>standard input</div><div class="output-file output-standard"><div class="property-title">output</div>standard output</div></div><div><p> </p><div class="epigraph"><div class="epigraph-text"><span class="tex-font-style-it"><a href="https://hearthstone.blizzard.com/en-us/cards/50071-mass-hysteria">Mass Hysteria</a> (Hearthstone)</span></div><div class="epigraph-source">$$$\color{white}{\text{You are sus}}$$$</div></div><p>Franklin is plotting a Christmas attack where he casts the spell <span class="tex-font-style-it">Mass Hysteria</span> on a village of elves.</p><p>There are $$$n$$$ elves numbered $$$1$$$ to $$$n$$$, where elf $$$i$$$ has a health value of $$$h_i$$$ and an attack value of $$$a_i$$$. <span class="tex-font-style-bf">Initially, $$$h_i=a_i$$$ for all $$$i$$$, and all $$$a_i$$$ are distinct.</span> Elf $$$i$$$ is alive if and only if its health is positive (i.e., $$$h_i \gt 0$$$).</p><p>When Franklin casts <span class="tex-font-style-it">Mass Hysteria</span>, the following process is repeated: </p><ul> <li> Choose a pair of distinct living elves $$$x$$$ and $$$y$$$ ($$$h_x,h_y \gt 0$$$) such that elf $$$x$$$ has <span class="tex-font-style-bf">not</span> attacked before. If no such pair exists, terminate the process. </li><li> Then, elf $$$x$$$ attacks elf $$$y$$$, decreasing $$$h_y$$$ by $$$a_x$$$. Additionally, due to recoil, $$$h_x$$$ is decreased by $$$a_y$$$. Note that $$$a_x$$$ and $$$a_y$$$ remain unchanged. </li></ul><p>The process will repeat until it is impossible to choose a valid pair of elves. It can be shown that <span class="tex-font-style-it">Mass Hysteria</span> terminates after at most $$$n$$$ iterations.</p><p>Given an integer $$$m$$$, construct a valid sequence of attacks such that exactly $$$m$$$ elves are alive when the process ends; or determine that no such sequence exists.</p></div><div class="input-specification"><div class="section-title">Input</div><p>Each test contains multiple test cases.
